Questions

The Ones I'd Expect You To Have

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Do I need an existing audience first?

No. Your name, Susan's, the Sotheby's affiliation, and your direct network are the audience. The first mastermind sells off the existing network. A typical first mastermind for an operator at your tier sells 12–20 seats off cold-warm outreach to existing network plus one round of paid acquisition. Audience-building runs in parallel, in the background, for Phase 2 and 3, but Phase 1 doesn't depend on it.

How much time does this require from me?

One day of filming per month. Two days for the in-person event. The rest is calendar holds and intro emails. Francis leads creative direction, ideation, and scripting (with production running through his videographer network). Andrei's side handles all funnel, copy, ad, and conversion work. Most operators at your stage spend less than 10 hours per month on the system once it's built. You don't become a content creator. You stay a broker who happens to also have an info business.

Does this conflict with the brokerage or Sotheby's?

No. The mastermind audience is other ambitious agents, not your buyers. There is zero competitive overlap with Sotheby's deal flow. If anything, the visibility lifts the brokerage too, because every public-facing asset reinforces your numbers and your specialty. Several agents who've built systems like this have actually seen meaningful lift to their listing business, because the public-facing teaching content acts as a credibility halo for prospective sellers and buyers as well.

How quickly is there real revenue?

First mastermind: 30–45 days from kickoff. First mentorship cohort: month two onward. Webinar and course revenue: month three onward, ramping into month six. The accelerator is the in-person event because seats sell faster off a fixed date than open enrollment. Phase 2 community converts off the mastermind room, which is why Phase 1 is the wedge.

What about December through February when the market goes quiet?

This is the entire reason to build it. Real estate slows. Info offers don't. The mentorship cohort that starts in November pays in advance and runs through Q1. The mastermind for spring is pre-sold in December and January. The webinar funnel runs continuously. Year-round revenue is the unlock, and it lands in your account during the months your peers are watching the calendar.

What if the first mastermind doesn't fill?

We design Phase 1 around the existing demand we can actually see — your direct network, Susan's network, the agents already inside Sotheby's affiliated offices, and your warm LinkedIn audience. That's why it sells. But if the first launch under-fills (say, 8 confirmed instead of 12), we don't push the event into a half-empty room. We pause, restructure outreach, push the date by 30 days, and run again with a sharper angle. The build doesn't continue billing while the event isn't running. If after a second attempt it's clear the audience isn't there at the price tier we modeled, we restructure the offer from the bottom up before continuing to Phase 2.

What if I want to stop after Phase 1?

That's an option. Phase 1 is the wedge, and it's also the natural stopping point if you decide you don't want a Phase 2 mentorship and Phase 3 webinar/course on top of it. After Phase 1 you own the recordings, the email list, the brand video, the deck, and the playbook. You can run another mastermind yourself a year later, or never run another one again. We build Phase 1 to stand alone in case the rest of the plan stops being interesting to you.

Why not just build YouTube and Instagram first?

Audience-building from scratch is a 12-to-24-month exercise. High-ticket sells off an existing network in days. Phase 1 doesn't need a million followers. It needs the right twenty seats sold to people already in your contact list. Phase 3 benefits from social, and we build it in the background while Phase 1 and 2 produce revenue. By month six the social and YouTube presence is mature enough to drive a meaningful share of cohort enrollment. By year two it becomes the primary acquisition channel.
