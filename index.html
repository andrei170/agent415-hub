Questions

The Ones I'd Expect You To Have

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Do I need an existing audience first?

No. Your name, Susan's, the Sotheby's affiliation, and your direct network are the audience. The first mastermind sells off the existing network. Audience-building runs in parallel, in the background, for Phase 2 and 3.

How much time does this require from me?

One day of filming per month. Two days for the in-person event. The rest is calendar holds and intro emails. Francis's team handles all production. Andrei's side handles all funnel, copy, and ad work. You don't become a content creator. You stay a broker who happens to also have an info business.

Does this conflict with the brokerage or Sotheby's?

No. The mastermind audience is other ambitious agents, not your buyers. There is zero competitive overlap with Sotheby's deal flow. If anything, the visibility lifts the brokerage too, because every public-facing asset reinforces your numbers and your specialty.

How quickly is there real revenue?

First mastermind: 30–45 days from kickoff. First community subscriptions: month two. Webinar and course revenue: month three onward, ramping into month six.

What about December through February when the market goes quiet?

This is the entire reason to build it. Real estate slows. Info offers don't. Community dues collect and the webinar funnel keeps selling through the trough. Year-round revenue is the unlock.

Why not just build YouTube and Instagram first?

Audience-building from scratch is a 12-to-24-month exercise. High-ticket sells off an existing network in days. Phase 1 doesn't need a million followers. It needs the right twenty seats sold to people already in your contact list. Phase 3 benefits from social, and we build it in the background while Phase 1 and 2 produce revenue.
