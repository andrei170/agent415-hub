01

Research Snapshot

Five content accelerators, publicly observable

The Five Accelerators

  • The $181M proof isn't in the IG bio. The single most credentialing line in luxury RE is missing from the surface that new visitors see first.
  • LinkedIn is dormant. The platform where Bay Area HNW buyers live, and it's running on listing announcements.
  • No listings-to-content pipeline. Every listing is a content factory waiting. None of it is being compiled.
  • YouTube long-form is unclaimed. No "CJ Nakagawa" channel. Neighborhood tours and market commentary have almost no serious competitor in the Marin + SF luxury tier.
  • No off-market capture. The single highest-value asset in luxury RE, and there is no way for a new visitor to opt in to receive it.

The Biggest Fix

Build the listings-to-content pipeline. Every new listing produces a Reel, a LinkedIn post, a YouTube walkthrough, a neighborhood explainer, and a buyer guide. The raw material is already being created. It just needs compiling and distribution.

06

Content Engine Framework

Listings-first, one week per month

The Engine

  • Input: Each new listing CJ takes.
  • Output per listing: One Instagram Reel, one LinkedIn long-form post, one YouTube walkthrough (2-4 min), one neighborhood explainer (short), one buyer guide email.
  • Cadence: One filming day per month covering 3-5 active listings. Rest of the month is editing, scheduling, and distribution.
  • YouTube channel: CJ Nakagawa personal channel. Neighborhood tours (Tiburon, Belvedere, Mill Valley, Pacific Heights). Quarterly market updates. Cube 3 development diaries.
  • LinkedIn cadence: Two posts per week, market analysis and anonymized deal stories.
  • Retargeting layer: Small paid boost on top-performing organic posts to HNW Bay Area lookalikes.

Short Positioning Video

3-5 minute video outline lives above the fold on the hub. Opens with the $181M number, covers heritage, specialty, how we work, proof. Replaces "hi welcome to my website" with hard proof in the first 30 seconds.
